Online Consent

This software is part of a research study conducted by Steven Gardiner, Anthony Tomasic, and John Zimmerman at Carnegie Mellon University. Participation in the study is optional, and is not required to use the software.

The purpose of the research is to determine how everyday users can effectively collaborate with a software agent to construct reusable scrapers for web tables.

Procedures

Participants will interact with the software tool. In preparation for using the tool, participants have the opportunity to view a brief video depicting its usage. Completion of a task usually takes less than 15 minutes.

Participant Requirements

Participation in the study is limited to individuals age 18 and older, and to individuals willing to use the Firefox browser and install an extension in the browser.

Risks

The risks and discomfort associated with participation in this study are no greater than those ordinarily encountered in daily life or during other online activities.

Benefits

There may be no personal benefit from your participation in the study but the knowledge received may be of value to humanity.

Compensation & Costs

Those using the software to complete Mechanical Turk HITs will receive the compensation as described in the HIT through the Mechanical Turk site.

There will be no cost to use the software nor to participate in the study.

Confidentiality

The data captured for the research do not include any personally identifiable information about you except your IP address and, if you are using Mechanical Turk, your Mechanical Turk worker ID.
